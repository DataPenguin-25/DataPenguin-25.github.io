Instacart Customer Behaviour Analysis

Tools: Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, SciPy), Jupyter Notebook

Project Overview

Instacart, a prominent online grocery platform, sought to refine its marketing strategies by gaining deeper insight into customer purchasing patterns and product demand. This comprehensive analysis examined over 32 million transaction records to uncover behavioural trends, identify distinct customer segments, and provide actionable recommendations for targeted marketing initiatives.

The project encompassed the complete data analytics workflow, from initial data acquisition and rigorous quality assurance through advanced feature engineering and customer profiling, culminating in strategic recommendations grounded in empirical evidence.

Analytical Process

Data Acquisition and Initial Assessment: Imported six distinct datasets comprising orders, products, departments, aisles, order details, and customer information. Conducted preliminary exploration to understand data structure and establish the analytical foundation.
Data Quality Assurance: Executed comprehensive consistency checks across all datasets. Identified and addressed 16 missing product names and 5 duplicate product records. Discovered 206,209 records with missing "days since prior order" values, which upon investigation corresponded precisely to first time orders. Created a boolean flag to preserve this valuable customer acquisition data.
Data Integration: Performed multiple merge operations to consolidate the disparate datasets. The primary merge combined order details with product information, yielding 32,404,859 matched records. Subsequently integrated customer demographic data for 206,209 unique customers.
Feature Engineering: Derived multiple analytical variables to support customer segmentation. Created temporal flags identifying busiest days (Saturday and Sunday) and peak ordering periods (10:00 to 16:00). Developed price categorisation labels distinguishing low range (under $5), mid range ($5 to $15), and high range (over $15) products.
Customer Behaviour Profiling: Utilised the transform() function to calculate user level aggregations whilst preserving the granular transaction structure. Engineered six behavioural flags: maximum order count, loyalty classification, average purchase price, spending category, median days between orders, and order frequency classification.
Regional and Demographic Analysis: Assigned customers to four geographic regions based on state data. Created comprehensive customer profiles combining age, income, and family status dimensions. Removed personally identifiable information prior to final analysis to ensure data security compliance.

Key Findings

Temporal Ordering Patterns

Analysis of ordering behaviour across the 24 hour cycle revealed distinct patterns in customer activity. Orders concentrate heavily during daytime hours, with peak activity occurring between 10:00 and 15:00.

Figure 1: Order volume peaks between 10:00 and 15:00, with minimal activity from 02:00 to 06:00

Insight: The concentration of 18.7 million orders during peak hours (10:00 to 16:00) compared to just 887,000 during quiet periods (00:00 to 07:00) suggests optimal windows for promotional campaigns and operational scheduling.

Customer Loyalty Distribution

Segmentation of customers based on lifetime order frequency revealed that Regular customers represent the largest segment, followed by Loyal customers and New customers. This distribution indicates that the majority of Instacart's transaction volume derives from established, returning customers.

Figure 2: Regular customers generate the highest order volume, demonstrating strong customer retention

Customer Profile Segmentation

Comprehensive customer profiling based on age, income, and family status identified six distinct segments. Seniors with Dependents emerged as the dominant profile, followed by Established Families. These family oriented segments collectively represent the majority of transaction volume.

Figure 3: Seniors with Dependents and Established Families represent the largest customer segments

Regional Profile Distribution

Analysis of customer profiles across geographic regions revealed consistent patterns. Seniors with Dependents represent approximately 37% of customers in each region. Established Families maintain a steady 23 to 24% share across all regions, suggesting marketing strategies can be applied nationally with regional fine tuning.

Figure 4: Customer profile distribution remains remarkably consistent across all four U.S. regions

Spending Behaviour Analysis

A significant disparity emerged in average product prices across customer segments. Loyal customers demonstrated a mean purchase price of $10.39, whilst new customers averaged $13.29 per product. This $2.90 differential suggests that established customers prioritise value and everyday essentials, whereas new customers may experiment with premium offerings.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Targeted Promotions: Launch marketing campaigns during peak hours (10:00 to 15:00) when customer engagement is highest to maximise conversion rates
  • Family Focused Marketing: Direct family oriented product promotions toward Seniors with Dependents and Established Families segments, which collectively dominate the customer base
  • Loyalty Programme Enhancement: Develop value bundles and rewards for loyal customers who demonstrate consistent, value focused purchasing behaviour
  • New Customer Strategy: Utilise premium product recommendations to attract new customers, capitalising on their higher average purchase price
  • Operational Efficiency: Schedule restocking and maintenance activities during low traffic evening and early morning hours
  • Regional Consistency: Apply national marketing strategies with confidence, as customer profile distributions remain consistent across all regions

Technical Implementation

This project comprised 11 Jupyter notebooks following a structured analytical workflow. Key technical approaches included:

  • Utilised pandas merge operations with indicator flags to verify data integrity across multiple joins
  • Applied groupby with transform() to calculate user level metrics whilst preserving transaction level granularity
  • Implemented loc() based conditional logic for creating categorical variables and customer flags
  • Created 70/30 train test splits using NumPy random sampling for visualisation validation
  • Developed custom functions for regional assignment and customer profile classification
  • Exported intermediate results as pickle files to maintain data types and optimise processing efficiency
