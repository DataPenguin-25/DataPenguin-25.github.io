Rockbuster Stealth Video Rental Analysis

Role: Data Analyst Tools: PostgreSQL, Tableau, Excel Database: 15 Tables, 599 Customers, 1,000 Films
View Tableau Dashboard → View GitHub Repository →

Project Overview

Rockbuster Stealth, a video rental company with a global customer base, sought to transition from physical stores to an online streaming model. The management team required data-driven insights to understand their customer distribution, revenue performance, and market opportunities across international operations. This project analysed their relational database using SQL to answer key business questions and inform the streaming launch strategy.

The Challenge

Rockbuster's management posed five critical business questions:

  1. Which movies contributed the most and least to revenue?
  2. What was the average rental duration?
  3. Which countries are Rockbuster customers based in?
  4. Where are customers with high lifetime value located?
  5. Do sales figures vary between geographic regions?

Data and Methodology

Database Structure

I connected to Rockbuster's PostgreSQL database, which utilised a snowflake schema comprising 15 related tables. The central fact table (payment) connected to dimension tables including customer, film, staff, store, and inventory, with geographic data normalised across address, city, and country tables. I created a comprehensive data dictionary and entity-relationship diagram to document the database architecture.

SQL Techniques Employed

The analysis required sophisticated SQL queries to traverse the normalised schema:

  • Multi-table JOINs: Connected four to five tables per query (customer → address → city → country → payment) to link customer data with geographic and transactional information
  • Common Table Expressions (CTEs): Organised complex logic into modular, readable components for identifying top countries and filtering high-value customers
  • Subqueries: Nested queries for comparative analysis and conditional aggregations
  • Aggregation Functions: Utilised SUM, COUNT, AVG, MIN, MAX, and MODE() to derive summary statistics
  • Performance Analysis: Compared CTE versus subquery execution using EXPLAIN ANALYZE

Key Findings

Global Customer Distribution

Analysis revealed that Rockbuster's 599 customers (584 active, 15 inactive) span multiple continents, with significant concentration in Asia. The top five countries by customer count were:

  • India: 60 customers generating $6,034.78 in revenue
  • China: 53 customers generating $5,251.03 in revenue
  • United States: 36 customers generating $3,685.31 in revenue
  • Japan: 31 customers generating $3,122.51 in revenue
  • Mexico: 30 customers generating $2,984.82 in revenue

High-Value Customer Analysis

The top five customers by lifetime value contributed $995.96 in total revenue, averaging $199.19 per customer. Notably, these high-value customers were geographically dispersed across Réunion, the United States, Brazil, the Netherlands, and Belarus, rather than concentrated in the highest-volume markets.

Film Catalogue Insights

The 1,000-film catalogue showed consistent pricing and duration patterns:

  • Average rental rate: $2.98 (range: $0.99 to $4.99)
  • Average replacement cost: $19.98
  • Average film length: 115 minutes
  • Most common rating: PG-13

Performance Optimisation

When comparing query approaches, CTEs demonstrated improved performance over nested subqueries. The CTE version executed in 21.2ms compared to 32.0ms for the equivalent subquery, a 34% improvement. While PostgreSQL optimises both approaches similarly, CTEs offered superior readability and maintainability.

Visualisation

I developed an interactive Tableau dashboard displaying global customer distribution and revenue by country. The geographic visualisation enables stakeholders to quickly identify market concentrations and regional performance patterns.

Recommendations

Based on my analysis, I provided the following recommendations to Rockbuster management:

  1. Prioritise streaming launch in India, China, and the United States where customer concentration and revenue potential are highest
  2. Develop loyalty programmes targeting high-value customers regardless of geographic location, as top spenders are dispersed globally
  3. Continue curating PG-13 content to align with demonstrated viewer preferences
  4. Explore growth opportunities in emerging markets such as Brazil, the Indonesia, and the Philippines where customer bases already exist

Technical Deliverables

  • Entity-Relationship Diagram documenting all 15 tables and their relationships
  • Comprehensive Data Dictionary detailing columns, data types, and foreign key constraints
  • SQL query library answering each management question
  • Interactive Tableau dashboard for geographic analysis
  • Executive presentation summarising findings and recommendations
