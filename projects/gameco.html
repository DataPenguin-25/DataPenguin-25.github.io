GameCo Regional Sales Analysis

Role: Data Analyst Tools: Excel (Pivot Tables, Charts) Data: VGChartz (1980-2016)

Project Overview

GameCo, a video game company, needed to evaluate regional video game sales trends to optimise their marketing budget allocation. This project analysed historical sales data spanning nearly four decades to identify market shifts and growth opportunities across North America, Europe, Japan, and other regions.

The Challenge

GameCo's marketing team operated under the assumption that regional sales remained relatively stable over time. They required analysis to either confirm this assumption or provide evidence of significant market shifts that should inform budget decisions.

Key Questions

  1. Have regional sales proportions remained stable over time?
  2. When did total global sales peak, and what has happened since?
  3. Which regions show the strongest long-term performance?
  4. How should marketing budgets be adjusted based on these trends?

Data and Methodology

Data Preparation

I obtained the VGChartz dataset covering video game sales from 1980 to 2016. I validated the data for missing or duplicate entries and prepared it for analysis in Excel.

Analytical Approach

  • Descriptive Statistics: Calculated summary statistics for sales by region, platform, and year
  • Trend Analysis: Created time series visualisations to examine regional sales evolution
  • Comparative Analysis: Compared regional performance using absolute figures and percentage of global sales

Key Findings

Challenging the Stability Assumption

The analysis revealed that GameCo's assumption of stable regional sales was incorrect. Significant shifts occurred over the period studied.

Global Sales Peaked in 2008

Total video game sales reached their highest point in 2008 before entering a sustained decline. This peak coincided with strong console generations and preceded the rise of mobile gaming.

Regional Shifts After 2008

North America and Europe, historically the dominant markets, declined significantly after 2008. Meanwhile, Japan and other regions demonstrated steadier long-term performance, maintaining more consistent sales even as the overall market contracted.

Recommendations

  1. Acknowledge that regional performance varies significantly over time and should be monitored continuously
  2. Recognise that North America and Europe, while strong, are no longer as dominant as they once were
  3. Consider the steady performance of Japan and other regions when allocating marketing resources
  4. Adapt genre strategy to align with regional preferences

Reflections

This project demonstrated the importance of testing assumptions with data. The marketing team's belief in stable regional sales, while intuitive, did not hold up under analysis. The experience reinforced my appreciation for letting evidence guide business decisions rather than relying on conventional wisdom. Additionally, working extensively with Excel pivot tables and charts strengthened my ability to explore and communicate data efficiently.
